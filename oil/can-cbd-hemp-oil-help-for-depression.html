If you have no interest in activities that you have loved before or you feel constant sadness and longing, this may mean that you may suffer from depression.

Depression is a generic term for such diseases as permanent depressive disorder, postpartum depression, bipolar disorder, psychotic depression, seasonal affective disorder and manic-depressive disorder. According to the World Health Organization, more than 300 million people of all ages suffer from depression worldwide. Depression is the main cause of disability and is a significant factor in the global development of disease.

Symptoms of depression

As mentioned earlier, a feeling of emptiness or sadness that lasts longer than a few weeks can be a sign of depression. Other emotional symptoms include the following:

  • Loss of interest in hobbies
  • Extreme irritability about minor things.
  • Fixing what went wrong in the past
  • fProblems with anger management
  • Thoughts of suicide or death
  • Some physical symptoms include the following:
  • Unexplained pains
  • debilitating fatigue
  • Difficulty making decisions or concentrating
  • Increase or decrease in appetite
  • Insomnia or too much sleep.
  • Weight loss or weight gain

In children, depression can lead to their refusal to go to school and build up social connections. Adolescents may begin to avoid communicating with friends and peers, and also become overly negative. Depression can be much harder to detect in older people. Common symptoms may include nursing, sleep problems, or unexplained memory loss.

Causes and risk factors

There is no single cause of depression. Genetics, hormones and brain chemistry can play a role. Other risk factors for depression may include the following:

  • Alcohol or drug abuse.
  • Chronic diseases such as cancer, multiple sclerosis or diabetes
  • Some prescription drugs
  • Low self-esteem
  • Family history of depression
  • Sexual or physical abuse
  • Posttraumatic stress disorder, borderline personality disorder, anxiety disorder
