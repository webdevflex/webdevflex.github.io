Living with depression can make everyday activities feel so over bearing. You no longer enjoy activities that use to be fun and ones that you once enjoyed. With depression you may shy away from going out and being with people that you once enjoyed being with. Many other symptoms that you might have if you have depression include:

  • Fatigue – having symptoms of feeling tired and lifeless.
  • Restlessness and trouble sleeping – you may experience trouble falling asleep and waking up in the middle of the night only to toss and turn for hours.
  • Crying – Moodiness and crying for no apparent reason.

Losing your drive to live – thinking about suicide or feeling like you have nothing to live for. If you have this symptom then immediate medical attention should be sought after.

These symptoms of depression may be more likely if you have or are:

  • Overweight – Being overweight can cause a person to feel like they are losing control since they cannot control their weight, which can trigger depression.
  • Major surgery – Having any kind of surgery is bad enough but the likelihood that depression will set in when having major surgery such as heart surgery, kidney transplants and etc. So if you have had major surgery and symptoms of depression have come about then you may want to talk to your doctor.
  • Going through a divorce or a separation after a long-term relationship.
