How Addictive is Paxil?Buy Prozac, buy paxil , buy zoloft

So how addictive is Paxil? Accusations get thrown around so easily that it can be difficult to discern what is actually going on. Some people are so afraid of anything that appears to cause anything pleasurable that they will cry addiction when people need it. However, it remains a question that should be answered. Paxil is currently one of the top drugs out there, able to handle a number of serotonin related conditions. People need to learn the truth about Paxil.

Asking people “how addictive is Paxil?” is actually misleading. It implies that it is addictive in the first place. However, nothing could be further from the truth. Not only is it not addictive, it actually cannot become an addictive substance.

First, people must understand what Paxil does. Paxil is a selective serotonin reuptake inhibitor. SSRIs, as they are also known, stabilize the chemicals in the brain, making sure that messages between nerve cells are sent. It does not add anything new, nor does it provoke the production of anything in the brain. It simply fixes what is already there to help treat things such post-traumatic stress disorder, various panic disorders, and depression. Off-label uses include treating dementia and obesity.

Those who call Paxil addictive might not know the definition of addiction. It is a psychological and physical dependence on a substance or an act. There are two kinds of addiction – behavioural and substance dependence. People who are addictive cannot decide for themselves, not well. They have to do things according to their addiction. They cease to function properly.

People can get addicted to a great deal of substances and to just about any act that they perform habitually. Gambling is a great example of an addictive habit. Understanding any sort of addiction relies on knowing why people get addictive. In Paxil’s case, as a substance, any addiction to it would result in what it does to the brain and how people feel when they take it.

Various substances can become addictive by virtue of changing how the brain works. Tobacco, alcohol and various drugs all do that. Paxil does not change how the brain works. Rather, it makes sure that the brain actually does what it is supposed to do. It does not control it or provoke it in any meaningful way. All it does is makes sure that the brain’s nerve cells can communicate with each other.

Paxil, interestingly, is not a commonly abused drug simply because it does not offer any sort of gratification, whether instant or delayed. Necessity is not addiction. People must take it, according to doctors, because they may suffer from a relapse if they stop suddenly. That is not addiction. That is like saying people are addicted to food because they say they would die without it.

Paxil is an anti-depressant with a multitude of uses. It may cause withdrawal, but it is not because someone was addicted to it. It is something that returns a person to normal, rather than gets them high. Paxil is an effective anti-depressant, but it is not addictive.
