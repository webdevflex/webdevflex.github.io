Fluoxetine is the generic brand for Prozac. It is used to treat depression, bulimia, OCD (obsessive compulsive disorders, and severe PMS (premenstrual syndrome). Fluoxetine helps control premenstrual syndrome symptoms like irritability, mood swings, and tension. In addition, Fluoxetine can help with the bloating and tenderness experienced with premenstrual syndrome. Prescriptions like Fluoxetine helps by increasing the serotonin available within the patient’s brain.

To take Fluoxetine, you usually ingest the medication orally usually once in the moring with either food or water. Your dosage will be based on how severe your condition stands and your response to therapy. Your dosage and your schedule to take Fluoxetine is determined by the doctor. Always take all of your medication as prescribed and continue taking it even if you feel better. You should never discontinue use of any medication without the permission of the doctor. It may take up to four weeks before you begin to feel the befits of Fluoxetine.

Fluoxetine may cause nausea, headache, insomnia, dry mouth, drowsiness, sweating, or upset stomach. Of course, if any of your symptoms persist or get worse, consult your doctor immediately. Make contact with your doctor if any of the following symptoms occur: weight loss, appetite loss, severe or unusual mood swings, tremors, decreased interest in sex, flu-like symptoms, fever, chills, aches, and weakness. Some other rare side effects are vision changes, trouble swallowing, swelling of mouth or face, sexual inability, prolonged and painful erection, fainting, and change of heart beat.

An allergic reaction is very unlikely but is possible. Always seek immediate medical attention if you have the following symptoms: rash, itching, dizziness, weakness, breathing troubles, and any swelling. You can contact either your doctor or your pharmacist if you have any questions about any of the side effects or the drug Fluoxetine

Use extreme caution if you have diabetes, liver disease, or any alcohol or drug dependency. It is suggested that you take extreme caution with Fluoxetine if you are in your elder years because your side effects could be more severe. This should only be used during a pregnancy when it is absolutely nessassary. If you are pregnant or breast-feeding consult your doctor’s opinion.

Fluoxetine has fatal reactions to a long list of other drugs. It is cruicual that you notify your doctor of any medication that you are taking, even if it’s a non-prescribed medication or vitamin.
