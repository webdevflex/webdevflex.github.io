Most people take Zoloft orally and usually once a day. You can take Zoloft with or without food. Always take any medication as directed by your doctor. It is suggested that you take your dosages at the same time every day. You should take your Zoloft in the morning or in the evening. The dosage is based on your response to therapy as well as other factors. It is important for you to continue Zoloft and other medications even if you feel better. You should never discontinue the use of any medication without consulting your doctor first. It could take up to four weeks before you feel the full benefit and effects of Zoloft.

Zoloft is used to treat depression, anxiety, post-traumatic stress disorder and OCD (obsessive compulsive disorders). It has also been used to treat extreme premenstrual syndrome and sexual dysfunctions (premature ejaculation). Zoloft works by restoring the balance of chemicals within the brain.

Some of the side effects of Zoloft are nausea, headache, diarrhea, insomnia, dry mouth, drowsiness, dizziness, and upset stomach. If any of these side effects continue or worsen notify your doctor promptly. You should tell your doctor immediately if you experience the following side effects: loss of appetite or weight, unusual mood swings, sweating, fatigue, tremors, decreased interest in sex.

Tell your doctor immediately if you have vision changes, changes in sexual ability, painful or prolonged erection, fainting, changes in heartbeat, chest pain, muscle pain, trouble swallowing, ears ringing, and seizures. Allergic reactions to Zoloft are unlikely but possible. If you feel that you are having an allergic reaction, seek immediate medical attention. Symptoms of an allergic reaction might include rash, itching, swelling, dizziness, and trouble breathing. You may notice other side effects not listed, you may contact your doctor or pharmacist with any questions about Zoloft that you might have.

Make sure that you tell your doctor of your complete medical history. If you have liver, kidney, or heart problems Zoloft might not be for you. Tell your doctor of any allergies, dizziness or drowsiness, or seizures that you have. Zoloft can make you dizzy or drowsy so use caution when operating heavy machinery.

Limit alcoholic beverages since alcohol is a depressant. You should use caution when using Zoloft with the elderly because they are more sensitive to drugs and their side effects. Zoloft should not be used unless absolutely needed during a pregnancy. Zoloft passes into breast milk, so you should not breast feed while using Zoloft.
