Why You Should Take Accutane

Accutane (Isotretinoin) is known acne medication that is deemed as an extremely potent drug that treats nodular or cystic acne. Although the drug has several side effects from dryness of the skin to headaches, taking Accutane has a number of benefits too. This includes reducing oil glands on the skin and decreasing oil production as a whole.

Sebaceous glands are responsible for providing the skin with natural oil keeping the skin moisturized, soft, and supple. Too much of it however can result to the development of pimples and in some cases of acne. How come this happens? This is a common question because oil, after all, is supposed to be a good thing especially if it makes the skin look better.

However, one has to keep in mind that there is bacteria called the P. acnes that is produced together with natural oil on the skin and the more oil there is, the more bacteria will be activated.

To control the production of facial oil, isotretinoin is given by dermatologists. This basically helps in reducing oil production, reducing the number of pimples of the user along the way.

The drug is also effective in reducing inflammation specifically inflamed acne nodules that is why it is quite popular among dermatologists and individuals who suffer from severe acne conditions. Aside from getting rid of inflammation, this benefit of taking isotretinoin also results to less scarring on the skin.

Since nodular acne often leave ugly marks due to collagen disruption and occupying far too much space by inflaming the pores, the reduction of inflammation on pimples helps in keeping the pores intact and collagen undisrupted which leads to little to no scars.

Another benefit of using isotretinoin is that it slows down skin cell production. Too much of it can clog the pores which later lead to acne breakouts. According to dermatologists, the buildup of skin cells also entails the buildup of bacteria. The latter later on results to inflamed pores i.e. pimples.

The slowing down of skin cell production on the skin is important for the treatment of severe acne because this helps clear the skin. Doctors say that there is a need to slow it down so that isotretinoin or Accutane can catch up with the healing of inflamed pores.

There are a lot of benefits one can get from Accutane but there is still a need to consult your doctor before taking it.
